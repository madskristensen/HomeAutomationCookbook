# Smart devices guide

Choosing the right smart home devices can feel overwhelming with hundreds of options available. This guide breaks down each device category, explains what to look for, and helps you make informed decisions based on your specific needs and budget.

<div class="category-intro">
  <h2>Before you start</h2>
  <p><strong>Start with sensors:</strong> Sensors are the foundation of any smart home. They detect what's happening and trigger automations. Without sensors, you're just controlling devices manually through an app.</p>
  <p><strong>Buy compatible devices:</strong> Check that devices work with your chosen platform before purchasing. Most manufacturers list compatibility on their packaging.</p>
  <p><strong>Prefer local control:</strong> Devices that work locally continue functioning when your internet goes down. Cloud-only devices become useless without connectivity.</p>
</div>

## Connectivity standards

Understanding how devices communicate is crucial for building a reliable smart home. Each protocol has trade-offs between ease of setup, reliability, and power consumption.

<div class="platform-grid">
  <div class="platform-card">
    <h4>WiFi (2.4GHz)</h4>
    <p><strong>Best for:</strong> Simple setups, renters, small homes</p>
    <p>Connects directly to your router with no hub needed. Easy to set up but can overwhelm networks with many devices. Higher power consumption means smart bulbs need constant power.</p>
    <p><strong>Examples:</strong> TP-Link Kasa, Wyze, LIFX</p>
  </div>

  <div class="platform-card">
    <h4>Zigbee</h4>
    <p><strong>Best for:</strong> Larger homes, battery devices, reliability</p>
    <p>Low-power mesh network where devices relay signals to each other. Excellent for sensors and switches. Requires a Zigbee hub or coordinator. Very reliable once set up.</p>
    <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Philips Hue, Aqara, Ikea Tradfri</p>
  </div>

  <div class="platform-card">
    <h4>Z-Wave</h4>
    <p><strong>Best for:</strong> Maximum reliability, no interference</p>
    <p>Operates on a dedicated frequency with no WiFi interference. Mesh network with excellent range. More expensive devices but very reliable. Popular for security and locks.</p>
    <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Schlage, Inovelli, Zooz</p>
  </div>

  <div class="platform-card">
    <h4>Thread / Matter</h4>
    <p><strong>Best for:</strong> Future-proofing, cross-platform homes</p>
    <p>Thread is a low-power mesh protocol. Matter is an application layer for device interoperability. Both are promising but still maturing. Wait for broader adoption before investing heavily.</p>
    <p><strong>Examples:</strong> Eve, Nanoleaf, newer Apple/Google devices</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="info-box">
  <strong>A note on Matter and Thread</strong>
  <ul>
    <li>Thread is a networking protocol (like WiFi or Zigbee)</li>
    <li>Matter is an application layer that runs over Thread, WiFi, or Ethernet</li>
    <li>Both show promise but ecosystems are still evolving</li>
    <li>We recommend waiting before fully committing to Matter-only devices</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## What to check before buying

Before purchasing any smart device, run through this checklist to avoid compatibility issues and buyer's remorse.

<div class="setup-steps">
  <div class="setup-step">
    <h4>1. Platform compatibility</h4>
    <p>Does it work with your hub or ecosystem? Check the manufacturer's compatibility list. Look for native integration rather than workarounds.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="setup-step">
    <h4>2. Local vs. cloud control</h4>
    <p>Can the device work without internet? Local control means reliability during outages. Cloud-only devices are vulnerable to service shutdowns.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="setup-step">
    <h4>3. Power requirements</h4>
    <p>Does it need a neutral wire? Battery or hardwired? Check your home's wiring before buying smart switches. Older homes often lack neutral wires.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="setup-step">
    <h4>4. Physical fit</h4>
    <p>Will it fit your existing fixtures? Measure junction box depth for switches. Check bulb size and base type for smart bulbs.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="setup-step">
    <h4>5. Reviews and reliability</h4>
    <p>What do real users say? Look for reviews mentioning long-term reliability, not just initial setup. Check for firmware update history.</p>
  </div>
</div>
